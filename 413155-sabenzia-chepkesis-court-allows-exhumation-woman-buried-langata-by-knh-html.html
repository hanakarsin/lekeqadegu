Sabenzia Chepkesis: Court Allows Exhumation of Woman Buried at Lang'ata Cemetery by KNH

- Sabenzia Killong was interred at Lang'ata cemetery on April 27, without the family's approval leading to the tussle

- On Monday, May 24, Milimani court granted the family's request to exhume the body and conduct a postmortem

- After the autopsy, the family will rebury her remains in Ndalu location, Bungoma county, as per their wish

A Nairobi court has allowed a Bungoma family to exhume their kin buried in a mass grave at the Lang'ata Cemetery.

Sabenzia Killong was interred at the facility by Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) on April 27, without the family's approval leading to the tussle.

On Monday, May 24, Milimani Commercial Magistrate Court Principal Magistrate D W Mburu granted the family's request to exhume the body and conduct a postmortem.

After the autopsy, the family will rebury her remains in Ndalu location, Bungoma county, as per their wish, ending their six months' misery.

The family lawyer Ken Murunga said after Monday's ruling, the exhumation exercise might be carried out on Tuesday, May 25, after engaging all the respondents.

Disturb 18 others

Previously, the family's exhumation request had been dealt a blow after the Nairobi county said the move would disturb 18 others laid to rest alongside her.

The County Solicitor Eric Abwao said the deceased was buried in a mass grave, and as such, if her remains were to be exhumed, it would lead to a disturbance among members of the public.

“If the body of Sabenzia Chepkesis Killong was buried in the mass grave together with 18 other bodies as per exhibit TK4, then exhuming all the 19 bodies to take away one body shall be an exercise that may cause public nuisance against the provisions of the Public Health Act, Cap 242,” the county official stated in an affidavit.

Killong's illness, death and burial

Killong was admitted to KNH on December 20, where she succumbed to her illness after eight days.

Her body stayed in the mortuary for four months before the facility decided to bury her as per the Public Health Act at Lang'ata Cemetery.

The family claimed that the national health facility did not notify them of the death of their kin despite reporting the matter to two police stations and sharing her details at the time she was lying in the morgue.

Killong' sister named Joy, said they visited the facility severally to search for their kin, but the hospital claimed that they never admitted anyone by that name.
