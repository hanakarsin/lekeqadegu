New NHIF rates for employees and the self employed in 2024

National Insurance Fund (NHIF) is a state parastatal agency under the ministry of health. It accommodates various healthcare needs for qualified citizens. The fund’s principal mandate is to provide medical cover to its members and their stated dependents, such as children and spouses. In 2021, the agency changed its terms by incorporating new NHIF rates for salaried individuals.

NHIF membership is open to anyone who is Kenyan and is above the age of 18 years old. However, one should have a monthly income of more than Ksh. 1,000. With close to 60 branches across the country, the agency services are readily available to the majority of employees and members. Below is everything you need to know about NHIF rates and penalties.

NHIF rates for the self-employed

As mentioned, NHIF is open for all Kenyan 18 years and above, regardless of their employment status. Individuals in the informal sector, retirees, self-employed and voluntary persons are required to pay Ksh. 500 monthly or Ksh. 6000 annually.

NHIF benefits

NHIF is Kenya's largest, most affordable, reliable, and trusted medical insurance coverage. It has incredible covers, costing Ksh. 500 monthly for principal members and beneficiaries. Here are the various benefits that NHIF members have access to.

1. Inpatient benefit package

The inpatient comprehensive cover is available to all eligible NHIF members and their dependents. The benefit package includes the following;

  • General and specialist consultation fee
  • Prescription and dressings
  • Operating theatre charges
  • Diagnostic laboratory and radiology investigations
  • Nursing care
  • Bed charges
  • Procedure and treatment

2. Outpatient benefit package

This cover is available to all eligible NHIF members and their dependents. They include;

  • Medicine and dispensation
  • Laboratory and radiology investigations (including x-ray and ultrasounds)
  • Consultation
  • Day-care procedures
  • Behavioral health services
  • Counseling
  • Therapy services
  • Anti-snake venom and anti-rabies
  • Post-natal and antenatal care
  • Physiotherapy sessions
  • Immunization/ vaccines

3. Maternity benefit package

This package covers pregnancy-related health services. They include; aftercare for both mother and newborn, delivery through a cesarean section or normal delivery, and facilitation of labor.

4. Renal dialysis benefit package

The benefits package covers hemodialysis, including routine laboratory tests, dispensed and intra-care medications, dialysis, and nursing services, specialist review, follow-up, and counseling care.

5. Oncology benefit package

This package includes the treatment of cancer patients using immunotherapy, chemotherapy, brachytherapy, radiotherapy, pet scans, and other treatments as specified by the Ministry of Health.

6. Radiology benefit package

The package includes diagnostic imaging services for CT Scans and MRIs. Note that ultrasounds and x-rays are covered under outpatient and inpatient packages.

7. Emergency care benefits

It covers onsite evacuation and transfer of a sick NHIF member or their dependent to the nearest NHIF-accredited hospital. It also covers ambulance services.

8. Mental and behavioral health benefit package

The package covers neurological disorders (dementia and epilepsy), common mental disorders (anxiety and depression), severe mental disorders (bipolar, schizophrenia, ad psychosis disorder), substance abuse and disorders, and childhood disorders.

9. Surgical benefit package

It covers all surgical procedures, including transplants. However, it excludes cosmetic/ plastic surgeries.

10. Overseas treatment

This package covers all treatment costs for surgical and medical interventions unavailable in Kenyan hospitals.

Can I use the NHIF card in any hospital?

An NHIF member and their dependents aren't restricted to a specific hospital for inpatient services. It means you can access inpatient services in any hospital/ facility accredited by NHIF countrywide. But for outpatient, you'll be limited to the accredited hospital you selected while applying for NHIF.

NHIF penalties

Every registered self-employed NHIF member is required to contribute Ksh. 500 monthly. Existing members must submit their payments on or before the 9th of every subsequent month to avoid penalties. A payment made after the 9th will accrue a penalty of 50% (Ksh. 250) of the voluntary monthly contribution.

If an NHIF member defaults for over 12 months, they’re treated like new members. They'll have to pay Ksh. 1500, which acts as their three-month contribution, and then wait 2 months for their cover to be active. It's advisable to make timely monthly payments to avoid penalties.

The company/employer is mandated to pay their employee's monthly NHIF contribution. The NHIF payment by employers should be made on time, or else they shall be liable to pay an equal to 10% of the contribution amount.

Frequently asked questions

Here are the most common questions about NHIF.

How do I pay my monthly contribution?

Your employer is responsible for submitting the amount to NHIF every month. In case you are self-employed, you can pay using M-Pesa and other alternatives. You should use 200222 as the Pay Bill number and your ID number as the account number. Deposit Ksh. 500 or Ksh. 6,000 for a whole year.

Does NHIF pay for surgery?

Yes. The scheme caters for caesarean sections and any procedures involving organ transplants. What’s more, if you need specialized surgery from abroad that is not available in the country, all your medical bills will be paid.

What is NHIF Supa Cover?

It is the largest, most reliable, affordable, and most accessible medical cover that makes sure you and your family enjoy unparalleled benefits. For a small amount of money each month, all your medical bills will be covered by the scheme. Hence, you will be walking into specified hospitals and getting particular services without paying a cent.

How much is NHIF Supa Cover?

The insurance will cost you Ksh. 500 per month. The amount is supposed to be paid by the principal member. Note that the amount covers your dependents too. Couples should produce their marriage certificate for registration.

What does Supa Cover entail?

As a member of this scheme, you will have access to outpatient services and inpatient services, which are medical treatment procedures provided by a hospital or any other facility that requires at least an overnight stay.
