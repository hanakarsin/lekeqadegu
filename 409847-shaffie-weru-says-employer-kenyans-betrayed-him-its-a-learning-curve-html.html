Shaffie Weru Says Employer, Kenyans Betrayed Him: &quot;It's a Learning Curve&quot;

- Shaffie Weru said his case was rushed without a deep analysis of it

- He claimed that his colleagues could not assist as they seemed to have ganged with the online battalion to castigate him

- His also faulted his employer for giving in to the social media pressure, firing him without being given a chance to argue the case

Shaffie Weru has ultimately chested out regarding the fracas that saw him get the sack from Radio Africa Group, a company he had served for more than a decade.

Shaffie was sacked in March alongside DJ Joe Mfalme and Neville Muasya over allegations of propagating abuse to women through their sentiments that many found derogatory.

In an interview with KTN, the former radio host said that it all amounted to a kind of betrayal when no one, including his acquaintances, could be ready to deeply examine the basis of their conversation that caused the wild online frenzy.

"It happened so fast...they did not take time to find out what the story was all about. Only a part of the conversation was picked...my fans, friends and Kenyans betrayed me," said Shaffie.

During their breakfast show back in March, the trio, Shaffie, Mfalme, and Neville, had discussed the cases of gender abuse that had been constantly on the rise.

In the midst of their discussion, a reference was made to a story where a lady had survived after being pushed from a storey building, faulting the said lady for being "easily available" to men.

Their verdict that ladies had been easily available for men is what fired up the internet, netizens pushing for their dismissal.

While seemingly taking responsibility, Shaffie now says the timing of their on-air discussion is what could have fueled their tribulations.

"We can be faulted for the timing. When we did the conversation we had not thought about the drama and the similar dramas that had happened prior to our conversation," he said.

Whilst their discussion could have been valid, he said the time within which it happened was wrong given the rampant cases of gender-based abuse.

Commenting on the termination of his employment, Shaffie faulted his former employer for bowing to social media pressure and sanctioning his sacking without aptly probing the whole matter.

He thinks Radio Africa could have done better by educating him and generally its broadcasting staff about gender-related matters, in turn passing the knowledge to their audience.

The radio presenter believes the decision to sack him, and his colleagues, was inconsequential as it did not help solve the matter given the cases being reported on a regular basis.
