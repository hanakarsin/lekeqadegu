Sarakis Camp Speaks On His Son Spraying Dollar Notes On Stripper In Viral Video

The media group of former Senate President, Senator Abubakar Bukola Saraki has debunked viral report alleging that its principal’s son was caught spraying dollar notes on strippers.

The media team dismissed the report in a press statement issued on Friday from the office of the Director General of the Saraki National Media Group, Earl Osaro Onaiwu.

Osaro said that the reports were fabricated by deluded persons who are sponsored by failed politicians.

He also advised the public to discountenance the lewd video which it described as “expressions and vanity that hit the social media space earlier today, which mischief makers have purportedly claimed that the individual is a son of former Senate President, Dr. Abubakar Bukola Saraki.

“What I want to tell you categorically, is that, the individual in that video is neither Saraki’s son nor relation and so, he cannot be his son by any means or in any way.

“Sonship in any family is by consanguinity and birth. In this case, how can someone who’s not even related by blood and was not an adopted child be inserted into a family by rogue media trending?”

“I enjoin the media to cross-check their facts or do basic information inquisition to determine facts before rushing to ‘break lies instead of wholesome news.

“This will help to avoid the embarrassing realization that hearsay does not amount to truth, no matter the rumour industry perspectivizations of a blatant falsehood,” he advised.
