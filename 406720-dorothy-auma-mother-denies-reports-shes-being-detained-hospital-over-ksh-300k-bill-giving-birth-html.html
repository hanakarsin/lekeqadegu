Dorothy Auma: Mother Denies Reports Shes Being Detained in Hospital Over KSh 300k Bill after Givi

- Dorothy Auma denied reports alleging she had been detained in one of the hospitals in Migori county for over five months for failing to pay bill of KSh 300k after giving birth

- She also said bill accumulated to that amount due to number of operations she went through as a result of complications she developed after giving birth

- Auma said she was still under medications, and that was the reason why she was still at the hospital

A woman who was reported to have been detained in one of the hospitals in Migori county for failing to pay Ksh 300,000 hospital bill has denied the reports.

Reports by a section of the media had claimed that the mother, Dorothy Auma, had been detained at St. Joseph Ombo hospital for five months after giving birth for failing to settle the hospital bill.

However, speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Auma narrated that on October 29, 2020, she developed labour pains and went to the hospital where she delivered her baby through Caesarian Section, which later led to stomach complications.

She said after the birth, her wound burst open and she was taken for an operation and the same kept on happening, which forced her to stay longer in hospital.

The mother disclosed the operations had led to the bills accumulating, adding that in five months, she had undergone six operations.

The mother of five also revealed that she was scheduled to go through the seventh operation on March 7, but that did not happen due to the pending balance that she was owing the facility.

"After giving birth, I developed some complications which necessitated the operations, one after the other, and that is why I am still here because I cannot be released to go home while I am still sick," Auma said.

She also divulged that the health workers at the facility had been treating her well despite the money she owed the facility, adding that the hospital has been providing milk for her baby. She has not been breastfeeding the child due to the wound on her stomach.

"My baby has grown through the help of the nurses here. She is now in good health and very strong even though I didn't breastfeed her like my other four children due to my health," she said.

According to the hospital's administrator, Sister Beatrice Osire, the mother has not been able to pay the bills since she was using Linda Mama card, which cannot cater for the bills. She does not have an NHIF card.

The administrator said patient’s husband is also a peasant and he is the one who has been taking care of the other four children back home ever since the wife was admitted.

"It is true that she has a bill of KSh 300,000, but we have not detained her. She is still on medication and we totally understand, that is why we have been treating her even without the bill ," the administrator said.

Auma is appealing to well-wishers to help her raise the money to pay the hospital bill and also to enable her go through the operation that was skipped.

Any one who would wish to assist her can reach through to her phone number 0114573990.
