Millie Odhiambo says Raila ready to work with Ruto if he agrees to have handshake

- Millie Odhiambo argued Raila Odinga's supporters do not hate William Ruto and as such, the former PM would welcome an opportunity to join hands with him ahead of 2022

- The Suba North lawmaker dismissed the notion ODM members were following the former PM "blindly" and maintained they supported him because of the good things he had done for Kenya

- Raila had earlier said he could work with the DP if circumstances changed

Suba North Member of Parliament Millie Odhiambo has said former prime minister Raila Odinga is ready to work with his political nemesis William Ruto if the duo agree to have a handshake of their own.

ODM members don't hate Ruto

Speaking to Citizen TV in the night of Wednesday, January 13, the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) lawmaker argued Raila's supporters do not hate the second in command and as such, they will welcome an opportunity to join hands with him ahead of the 2022 General Election.

"We don’t hate William Ruto, we may not like what he espouses as a person but should William Ruto and Raila Odinga decide that they are having a handshake and are coming on an agenda that is sensible to us, we will work with them," the legislator said.

Odhiambo dismissed the notion ODM members were following Raila "blindly" and maintained they supported him because of the good things he had done for the nation.

"In ODM it’s Raila Amolo Odinga… we have trusted and loved him because of the good he has done for this country… we don’t follow him blindly," she opined.

Raila to work with Ruto

Earlier, as reported by TUKO.co.ke, Raila had said he could work with Ruto if circumstances conspired.

Speaking to KTN News on Wednesday, December 16, Raila said he had no personal problem with Ruto and dismissed claims he sacked him from the grand coalition government over corruption allegations.

"We actually did not chase Ruto from ODM, he is the one who left the party. The investigation into the maize scandal was not in any way a personal issue. I just interdicted him after the investigations, I did not sack him. "They say politics make strange bedfellows. So we have worked with Ruto before and if circumstances will conspire then I will have to work with him again," he said.
