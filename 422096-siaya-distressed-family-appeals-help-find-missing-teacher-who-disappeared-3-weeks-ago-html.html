Siaya: Distressed Family Appeals for Help to Find Missing Teacher Who Disappeared 3 Weeks Ago

  • George Adede went missing on the same day when schools were closed on July 15, 2021
  • According to his wife, Christine Anyango, he went home and freshened up before he stepped out to visit a friend
  • Anyango said her husband had not returned by the time curfew started, and when she contacted him, he promised he would be home soon
  • He, however, did not return, and efforts to reach him the following day hit a dead end as his phone had been switched off

A distraught family in Siaya County is searching for their kin, George Adede, who went missing three weeks ago and efforts to trace him have hit a dead end.

The primary school teacher was last seen on the same day schools closed on July 15, 2021, and his family says they are yet to find any leads to his disappearance.

According to Adede's wife, Christine Anyango, he came from school on the fateful day, after which he prepared himself and left to see a friend of his.

"They closed on June 15, and then he came home at around 3pm, after which he just prepared himself, took bath and left saying he was going to a friend of his," said Anyango.

Phone switched off

Hours later, the teacher at Orembe Primary School in Gem Sub-county had not returned, prompting his wife to call him to know his whereabouts since it was already curfew time.

"I asked him him, 'Baba Chris, where are you?' he told me he was in Gori at the market. I asked him what he was doing there yet it was curfew time. He told me 'I am just coming," added Anyango

Adede did not return home that night, and attempts to reach him the following day hit a brick wall as his phone was switched off.

The search begins

The family started searching for him in Bondo, Kinyani and Siaya, but they could not trace him, and later a missing person's report was filed at Kogelo Police Station.

The family, however, claims they have not received enough cooperation from the police and the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) in Siaya.

Adede's colleagues described the teacher as a devoted teacher who taught social studies, CRE and mother tongue and never missed class without permission.

The family of the 49-year-old teacher is now hoping their beloved will return home soon.

Anyone with information on Adede's whereabouts can report to the nearest police station.

Missing Maranda High School teacher found dead

The body of Kevin Otieno Ongoma was recovered from River Nzoia near Sio Port on Friday, October 23, bringing to an end a two-week search for the biology tutor.
