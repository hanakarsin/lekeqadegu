Man caught up in Gilgil traffic jam misses dowry payment ceremony

A man has missed a dowry function after staying on traffic snarl up at Gilgil Weigh Bridge in Nakuru for about 10 hours.

Antony Okumu who was heading to Ugenya for dowry payment function left Nairobi on Friday, August 2, at around 9pm and got stuck on the way hours later.

Okumu expected to arrive in Ugenya on Saturday, August 3, morning, to attend the function but this could not happen since he remained trapped in the snarl up up to as late Saturday midday, Standard reported.

"I left Nairobi on time but look, I cannot attend a family function that made me travel because of traffic snarl-up," said Okumu

The gridlock was caused by the ongoing construction of a section of the road at Gilgil Weigh Bridge.

Gilgil police boss Emmanuel Opuru said the situation was made worse by impatient motorists who disobeyed traffic officers and started overlapping and overtaking randomly causing the snarl up.

Many more passengers who were traveling to attend to different issues were affected by the jam and forced to cancel their plans.

So bad was the traffic that it became difficult for one to either move forward to upcountry or turn back to Nairobi.

"I am not sure whether I will travel to the village or should get back to Nairobi because I am expected to report back at work on Monday morning," said one of the affected passengers
