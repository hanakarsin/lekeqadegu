Dinka tribe: Meet some of the tallest people in Africa

- The Dinka people are one of the tallest people in Africa with most of the men above 5ft tall

- Reports have it that there is a strong community of the Dinka people in the diaspora

- In the precolonial era, they lived mostly in farms and make ends meet through their livestock

The Dinka people, a Nilotic tribe from South Sudan are known as some of the tallest people in Africa. The Tutsi of Rwanda is another. They live along the Nile in Bahr el Ghazal and Dinka Ngok of Abyei Area in South Sudan.

According to oral traditions, the tribe originated from the Gezira in a place that is now known as Sudan. It should be noted that the Dinka people live mostly on farmland as they depend mostly on livestock for their livelihood.

The population census conducted in 2008 in Sudan showed that the Dinka people are about 4.5 million people, putting that at 18% of the country’s population and the biggest tribe in the nation. How Africa reports.

Another thing worthy of note is their language is in Latin letters with some exported characters.

Their average male height is 182.6cm (5ft 11.9in) according to a survey done among 52 Dinka men between 1953 to 1954. In 1995, another survey showed it to be 5ft 9.4in.
