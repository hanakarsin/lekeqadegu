Senator Irungu Kang'ata says Uhuru, Raila's marriage is over: &quot;God is good&quot;

- Muranga Senator Irungu Kang'ata said Mt Kenya politicians had warned Uhuru against having a deal with Raila

- Kang'ata said Uhuru and Raila's marriage was over and it had vindicated Tanga Tanga politicians

- He said Mt Kenya politicians did not make a mistake to follow Ruto when he fell out with the president

Murang'a Senator Irungu Kang'ata has said the political marriage between President Uhuru Kenyatta and his handshake partner Raila Odinga is over.

The senator's sentiments came amidst rife speculation of Uhuru and Raila's fall out after Siaya Senator James Orengo lashed out at a section of government officials whom who claimed were hellbent on derailing the handshake.

Speaking on Sunday, March 7, Kang'ata said Mt Kenya politicians had warned Uhuru against his deal with the opposition leader.

“We warned President Uhuru not to enter any political partnership with Raila but he refused and the government started fighting us. Now he can see for himself,” he said.

He said the end of Uhuru and Raila had vindicated Tanga Tanga politicians who had doubts over the handshake and most importantly for sticking with Deputy President William Ruto.

“If we dared followed handshake and fought Ruto, we would now be isolated,” he added.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, Orengo, a close ally of Raila said there were 'cabals' inside government who had plans to hijack the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) for their own selfish interests.

Speaking in his Siaya backyard on Saturday, March 6, Orengo threatened to expose the civil servants whom he claimed had decided who will rule Kenya in 2022 at Raila's expense.

Orengo's remarks came after Raila's elder brother Oburu Oginga said the Opposition supremo may work with Deputy President William Ruto in the 2022 elections after rumours surfaced the duo could be talking behind the scenes.

Similarly, Kirinyaga woman representative Wangui Ngirici hinted that Ruto, the ODM leader and Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka may work together ahead of the 2022 General Election.
