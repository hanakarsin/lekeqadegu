Miguna Says Uhuru, Raila and BBI Proponents Should Be Charged for Flouting Constitution

- Lawyer Miguna Miguna said President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila Odinga knew the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process was illegal

- Despite this, the lawyer argued the two leaders pushed the document through, ignoring legal advice from critics

- He also called out lawyers who were involved in the process for knowingly supporting an illegal process

Exiled lawyer and author Miguna Miguna now wants President Uhuru Kenyatta and Orange Democratic leader (ODM) Raila Odinga charged for attempting to mutilate the constitution through illegal means.

His sentiments came a few hours after the High Court declared the Building Bridges Initiatives ( BBI ) unconstitutional, null and void.

Miguna said the promoters of the constitutional amendment bill led by the president and the former prime minister should be held accountable.

"Now we must arrest, fine and jail conman, Raila Odinga, despot Uhuru Kenyatta alongside their supporters like James Orengo, Atwoli and Junet," he tweeted on Thursday, May 13, night.

The lawyer argued the leaders involved knew the truth about the illegality of the BBI document but still pushed it through.

On the evening of Thursday, May 13, the High court said the BBI, a brainchild of the handshake truce between Uhuru and Odinga, was initiated by the head of state himself in contravention of the law.

In the ruling delivered by a five-judge bench, Joel Ngugi, George Odunga, Jairus Ngaah, Chacha Mwita, and Teresia Matheka declared the document unconstitutional.

"The BBI was the president's initiative which is contrary to Article 257 of the constitution. The task force that morphed to a steering committee was an unlawful and unconstitutional outfit. It was invalid from the beginning," the judges said.

They also faulted BBI proponents for their failure to avail adequate copies to the public, and added the electoral body did not ensure proper public participation in the process as it is constitutionally required.
