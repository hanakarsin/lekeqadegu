Tough Times for KQ Workers as Cash-Strapped Flag Carrier Extends Pay Cut Until Further Notice

  • KQ issued fresh 30% pay cuts in January to reduce operation costs and preserve cash
  • The airline, which posted KSh 11.48b in net loss in the six months ending June 2021, said the operating environment was still being negatively impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic
  • It had earlier said it needed KSh 54.9b for it to survive the turbulent 2021

Kenya Airways (KQ) will not restore the salaries of its workers to a 100%, at least in the near term, TUKO.co.ke has learnt.

The national carrier said salary pay cuts, which were effected at the height of the coronavirus pandemic, will remain for several months to come, as the airline struggles to preserve cash and cut costs.

Workers of the airline signed new salary pay cut contracts in January of up to 30 percent, which were intended to remain for a period of six to 12 months, subject to quarterly review.

"The operating environment since the outbreak of the pandemic has remained unchanged, adding that any changes would only be done once the situation improves," the loss making airline told Business Daily.

In the period ending in June 2021, KQ posted KSh 11.48 billion in net loss, marking a 19.8 percent drop from the KSh 14.33 billion loss posted in a similar period in 2020.

The airline had tripled its losses to KSh 36 billion for the 12 months to December 2020, when the pandemic heavily disrupted passenger levels.

Kenya Airways was still struggling before the emergence of the pandemic. In the year ending December 2019 for instance, the flag carrier posted losses of almost KSh 13 billion.

In an earlier report by TUKO.co.ke, the airline’s Chief Executive Officer Allan Kilavuka estimated that the airline would need about KSh 54.9 billion for it to survive the turbulent 2021, following the release of the full year 2020 financial results.

"The KSh 36.6 billion Kenya Airways loss is not a surprise, there is not an airline in the world that did not make a loss last year. KQ has the people to resolve the position and get better over time. We need KSh 54.9 billion dollars this year to survive," he said.

British Airlines workers, others also affected

Kenya Airways was not the only airline to issue pay cuts.

Several airlines among them British Airways, Delta Airlines Inc and Hong Kong’s Cathaway Pacific Airways also slashed their workers’ pay to stay afloat amid the pandemic.

As of July 2020, Bloomberg estimated that about 400,000 people had been fired, as airlines grounded their aircrafts owning to COVID related restrictions and lockdowns.
